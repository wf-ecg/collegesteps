Welcome to
CollegeSTEPS magazine!

Many of the strategies employed when I was planning and preparing for college were much different than those used today — yet the underlying fundamentals seem to have remained the same: plan early, apply for scholarships and grants, borrow only what you need, and try to stay within your budget.

From the beginning, Wells Fargo’s CollegeSTEPS® magazine has combined best practices, tested advice, and thoughtful information to serve as a valued college and financial planning resource for students, parents, school counselors, and others.

CollegeSTEPS magazine’s fresh approach to strategic planning will help you make choices you can feel good about, keep you mindful about the importance of budgeting, and ensure that you make the most of your college experience.

After witnessing the eldest of my three children move away to attend college and another getting ready to enroll as a freshman, I can attest that it takes an incredible amount of commitment, perseverance, and focus for students today to reach the milestone of becoming a first-year college student; for that you should always feel a sense of accomplishment.

Entering your college years is an important and positive milestone in life, of which I hope for you there will be many. For more information, I encourage you to visit wellsfargo.com/collegesteps , where you can sign up for ongoing tips and tools related to college planning and financing. Good luck and never stop pursuing your ambitions.

John Rasmussen
Executive Vice President
Wells Fargo Education Financial Services
